The sound

Eastern European fire, Taiwan dance-floor proof.

Led by Serbian guitarist and vocalist Marko Djurasić, Balkazar turns traditional Balkan songs and modern party arrangements into clapping, moving, dancing crowds. The set can shift from a loping ska-reggae groove into an odd-meter Balkan sprint, then land in rock, funk, Latin, or Slavic territory without losing the dance floor.

Shows & repertoire

Traditional songs, modern party arrangements, and controlled stage chaos.

A Balkazar night can pull from Balkan tradition, ska-reggae drive, world-fusion covers, and originals. Repertoire has included songs and influences such as Bubamara, Misirlou, Niška Banja, Bella Ciao, Istanbul, Dubioza Kolektiv-style party energy, Balkan Beat Box-style material, and more.

About

International players, Balkan roots, Taiwan party instinct.

Balkazar evolved from earlier Balkan-fusion projects in Taiwan into a dance-floor-first band with international members and a sound that treats rhythm as the main invitation. It is not a quiet background act — it is music for moving, shouting, clapping, and turning a room into a celebration.

The clean short version: Balkazar is a Balkan party band blending Eastern European, Gypsy, ska, reggae, rock, funk, Latin, Slavic, and world-music grooves into dance-driven live shows.
